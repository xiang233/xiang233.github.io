two poems on names

Posted on 2020-03-14 Edited on 2020-10-24

My father once gave me the entire universe.

I used it for a while until a FengShui master was visited,

With a stroke of the brush he clemently dissolved

The vastness and voidness,

Through clouds and ice, snows and sleet,

To the mildly drizzling rain,

And I wonder if after it the sun will shine again.

Today I return to that point, before and after.

Barely, rarely recalled, it seemed mostly forgotten.

Obscured. I pondered it over.

It didn’t turn out as I expected I would be,

shedding tears like the early morning dews,

but there were love out in torrents, permeating every soil of my soul.

Sometimes I felt pusillanimous.

Sometimes I felt magnanimous.

What resonance is there?

Does this begin to infuse into me,

Do I to this belong?

This melodious, early morning solace,

The Rain.

I was born and blessed with all of its gifts.

“Yao bequeathed Shun the world along with the jade”

The ether was touching its curve with a new charm,

And banishing for ever the traces of darkness.

“The flute when blown, mountains undulate.”

A low song of longing had been set singing

by the finery and beauty yet to be seen.

They, twinkling and tangling, but one,

Barely, rarely thought of, flashes in its modesty.

Ablaze. I let it in.

It was a little possessive,

As if asking for a private space but without my presence.

One for my father; the other for my mother.

One last to be mine, a gush of inward light that still shines.

What pulses are these?

Do they come to one in me,

Do I to it bind truly?

This abiding, ever-glowing possession,

My name.

The teacher asked us to each write a poem about our names, where they come from and what they mean to us. I wrote the first one for a friend and the second one for myself.
